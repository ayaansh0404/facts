Titanoboa

Titanoboa, the largest snake ever discovered, lived about 60 to 58 million years ago during the
Paleocene epoch. It could grow up to 12.8 meters (42 feet) long and
weigh between 730 and 1,135 kilograms (1,600 to 2,500 pounds). This prehistoric snake thrived in ancient tropical rainforests,
likely preying on large animals like fish and crocodiles.

Archaeopteryx

Archaeopteryx, a Jurassic-era animal about the size of a raven,

of bird-like and reptilian features
